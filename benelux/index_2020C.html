COVID-19: Train network in Benelux on Monday 23 March 2020

Do not travel unless you absolutely have to, e.g. if you have to travel to save lives! Practise social distancing. Due to the COVID-19 epidemic, train services have been gradually reduced since about Monday 16 March 2020 in and around Benelux. By Monday 23 March, the three countries have kicked in what seems to be the absolutely minimally required train schedules. Schedules are prone to changes quickly during this crisis; check with public transport providers repeatedly.

The following are the train network in Benelux on Monday 23 March 2020 in three maps. (Click on the thumbnails to open the pdf's.) Schedules for the following four weekdays are probably going to be similar to that of Monday 23 March 2020. (As for France, France has been telling people to check every single day. Both France and Germany have strict border restrictions.) In the Netherlands, Nachtnet trains are not running. In Belgium, P trains are very mostly not running. (The P train services that do run are not shown in the following maps.
