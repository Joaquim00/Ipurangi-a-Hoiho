Inner-Oceania flight network maps

'Inner Oceania' here refers to Oceania minus the better-connected New Zealand, Australia (see here for Norfolk Island), and Hawai'i. (I believe) All regularly-scheduled international passenger direct flights in and out of the airports in this region – for Northern Winter/Southern Summer 2016-2017 – are shown in this map. (Details will be checked again later as late 2016 approaches.) There are 31 airports in the region; they are shown as stars on the map (the two airports of Apia share one star). Destinations outside the region are shown as circles (or arrows if they are 'off the map').

Inter-connectivity within Inner Oceania is relative poor when compared to the Pacific Rim. The only roads that exist between the stars are between NAN and SUV, and between APW and FGI. International ferries are rare. The most practical way of travelling between the stars, or between a star and a circle, sometimes involves flights between the circles that are not shown in this map. If you are serious about travelling to/from/within Inner Oceania, it is best to consult flight search engines. This map is only for FUN. Perhaps for a network-theory exercise.
