Bicycle carriage rules: folded-up folding bicycles are treated as normal luggage, and can be taken for free in all hours in domestic trains(at the discretion of train conductors). For normal bicycles, all smaller operators seem to have the same rules as Nederlands Spoorwegen (including Breng), except for most lines on Arriva. NS bicycle day-tickets are valid in all domestic services listed below, unless otherwise noted.

  • Nederlands Spoorwegen: Bicycles allowed outside rush hour (06.30 – 09.00, 16.00 – 18.30), whole day during weekends and holidays, and the entirety of July and August, with a bicycle day-ticket. Not allowed on Intercity Direct (highspeed Amsterdam Centraal – Schiphol Airport – Rotterdam Centraal – Breda Centraal). Allowed on ICE travelling domestically (Amsterdam Centraal – Utrecht Centraal – Arnhem Centraal).
  • Breng: Same as NS.
  • Syntus: Same as NS.
  • Valleilijn: Seems to be the same as NS.
  • Veolia: Same as NS.
  • Arriva:
    • Groningen and Friesland (train lines to/from Groningen or Leeuwarden): Other than the normal NS bicycle day-ticket, there is a cheaper Arriva-only bicycle day-ticket, valid only for Arriva trains in this region. The time of validity is also different: the Arriva bicycle day-ticket is valid from 09:00.
    • Vechtdallijnen (Zwolle – Emmen, Almelo – Mariënberg): Same as NS.
    • Gelderland (train lines to/from Zutphen or Arnhem): Same as NS, except that bicycles can be taken for free during the weekend.
    • MerwedeLingelijn (Geldermalsen – Dordrecht): free/gratis! Train can take maximally 12 bicycles.
  • Also read about bicycle rules on international trains to/from the Netherlands.
  • Belgium: folding bike free, otherwise €5 one-way, €8 entire day in the entire Belgian network.
  • Germany... complicated.
