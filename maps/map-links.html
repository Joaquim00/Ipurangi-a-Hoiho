International passenger trains and Intercity Direct

Intercity Direct: Amsterdam Centraal – Schiphol Airport – Rotterdam Centraal – Breda Centraal. Surcharge of €2.40 for everyone between Schiphol Airport and Rotterdam Centraal.

Foreign local trains with stations in the Netherlands:

  • DB Regio NRW (Enschede – Enschede De Eschmarke – Glanerbrug to Dortmund Hbf/Münster (Westf) Hbf)
  • Eurobahn (Venlo to Düsseldorf Hbf and Hamm (Westf))
  • NMBS/SNCB (Maastricht – Maastricht Randwyck – Eijsden to Liège-Guillemins and Hasselt)

Long distance trains:

  • Intercity Berlin. Domestic travel on this train in the Netherlands (Amsterdam Centraal to Hengelo) is just like other domestic trains.
  • Intercity Brussels. Domestic travel on this train in the Netherlands (Amsterdam Centraal to Roosendaal) is just like other domestic trains.
  • ICE International: Amsterdam Centraal – Utrecht Centraal – Arnhem Centraal – Oberhausen Hbf – Duisburg Hbf – Düsseldorf Hbf – Köln Hbf – Frankfurt (Main) Flughafen Fernbahnhof – Frankfurt (Main) Hbf, and once per day further to Basel SBB. For domestic travel in the Netherlands, there is a surcharge of €2.40, unless you already have a subscription with NS, in which case there is no surcharge.
  • Thalys: Amsterdam Centraal – Schiphol Airport – Rotterdam Centraal – Antwerpen Centraal – Brussel Zuid/Bruxelles-Midi – [Paris Nord or Lille Europe]. Totatally different fare structure of its own. No Dutch domestic tickets works with Thalys.
    • Bicycle carriage rules

    Within the Netherlands, folded-up folding bicycles are treated as normal luggage, and can be taken for free in all hours in domestic trains. For normal bicycles, all smaller operators seem to have the same rules as Nederlands Spoorwegen (including Breng), except for most lines on Arriva. NS bicycle day-tickets are valid in all domestic services listed below, unless otherwise noted.

    • Nederlands Spoorwegen: Bicycles allowed outside rush hour (06.30 – 09.00, 16.00 – 18.30), whole day during weekends and holidays, and the entirety of July and August, with a bicycle day-ticket. Not allowed on Intercity Direct (highspeed Amsterdam Centraal – Schiphol Airport – Rotterdam Centraal – Breda Centraal). Allowed on ICE travelling domestically (Amsterdam Centraal – Utrecht Centraal – Arnhem Centraal).
    • Breng: Same as NS.
    • Syntus: Same as NS.
    • Valleilijn: Seems to be the same as NS.
    • Veolia: Same as NS.
    • Arriva:
      • Groningen and Friesland (train lines to/from Groningen or Leeuwarden): Other than the normal NS bicycle day-ticket, there is a cheaper Arriva-only bicycle day-ticket, valid only for Arriva trains in this region. The time of validity is also different: the Arriva bicycle day-ticket is valid from 09:00.
      • Vechtdallijnen (Zwolle – Emmen, Almelo – Mariënberg): Same as NS.
      • Gelderland (train lines to/from Zutphen or Arnhem): Same as NS, except that bicycles can be taken for free during the weekend.
      • MerwedeLingelijn (Geldermalsen – Dordrecht): free/gratis! Train can take maximally 12 bicycles.
    • Also read about bicycle rules on international trains to/from the Netherlands.
    • Belgium: folding bike free, otherwise €5 one-way, €8 for the entire day in the entire Belgian network.
    • Germany... complicated.
