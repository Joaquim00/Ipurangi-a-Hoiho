Benelux passenger rail network maps

The following are network maps of regular passenger train services in Belgium, the Netherlands, and Luxembourg in 2018. Different types of train services are presented on separate maps. Having them on separate maps makes them less messy and much easier to follow than the existing everything-in-one maps. However, the disadvantage is that... they are on separate maps; you might have to switch between maps to get a better idea on the interconnectivity between stations. There are separate maps for weekday vs. weekend schedules; the difference is more noticeable with the Belgian network.

There are no longer regular international night trains or autotrains in Benelux. Not included in the maps are seasonal trains to, e.g., Mediterranean destinations during summer, ski destinations during winter (some of which are night trains).

Unlike Germany, tickets for InterCity train services in Benelux fall in the same price range as local train services per distance. The same tickets are used, i.e., over the same trajectory, if there is a choice between InterCity and local train services, you can choose either service. Breaking of journey is allowed within the same day (usually till 04:00 the following day, according to the scheduled departure time).

High speed rail services have different pricing structures, and are much more expensive than other rail services. There are some exceptions. In the Netherlands, a) ICE between Amsterdam Centraal and Arnhem Centraal, and b) Intercity Direct between Schiphol Airport and Rotterdam Centraal, cost just the normal domestic price plus a prepaid surcharge of less than 3 euro (and the surcharge is waived in some cases). (The other sections of Intercity Direct cost just the normal domestic price.) Between Brussels and Paris, IZY is a low-cost alternative offered by Thalys, and it runs half on high-speed rail, and half on conventional rail. Except for Intercity Direct in the Netherlands, tickets for high-speed rail services are usually restricted to particular services, i.e. you cannot join an earlier or later train service than the one specified on the ticket. With some, seating reservation is obligatory.
