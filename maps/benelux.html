Benelux public transport network maps

The following are network maps of passenger train services in Benelux. InterCity and high speed rail services on one hand, and local train services on the other hand are presented in separate maps. While having them on separate maps makes the maps less messy, and hence much easier to follow, the disadvantage is that... they are on separate maps. The train services in Belgium differ greatly between weekdays and weekends, while there are also some less-drastic differences in the Netherlands and Luxembourg.

Unlike Germany, tickets for InterCity train services in Benelux fall in the same price range as local train services. High speed rail services have different pricing structures, and are much more expensive than other rail services. There are some exceptions. In the Netherlands, a) ICE between Amsterdam and Arnhem, and b) Intercity Direct between Schiphol and Rotterdam, is just the normal domestic price plus a (prepaid) surcharge of less than 3 euro (and the surcharge is waived in some cases). IZY is a low-cost alternative offered by Thalys between Brussels and Paris, and it runs about half the distance on high-speed rail, and the other half on conventional rail.
