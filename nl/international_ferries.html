International ferries

England across the North Sea/English Channel(from north to south):

  • IJmuiden (near Amsterdam) – Newcastle upon Tyne (England) with DFDS Seaway. Foot passengers and vehicles.
  • Hoek van Holland (near Rotterdam) – Harwich (England) with Stena Line. Foot passengers and vehicles. Also has a sail+rail deal with the Dutch railway network and/or the Greater Anglia network in England. Hoek van Holland terminal is served by the Hoek van Holland Haven train station, and Harwich terminal is served by the Harwich International train station.
  • Europoort Rotterdam – Hull (England) with P&O Ferries. Foot passengers and vehicles.
  • Zeebrugge (Belgium) – Hull (England) with P&O Ferries. Foot passengers and vehicles. (Walking from the ferry terminal to the two Belgian train stations in Zeebrugge not recommended; the port area is busy with heavy-duty trucks, and pedestrian facilities are not great.)
  • Dunkerque (Dunkirk; France) – Dover (England) with DFDS Seaway. Vehicles (incl. bicycles) and its passengers.
  • Calais (France) – Dover (England) with DFDS Seaway (Vehicles (incl. bicycles) and its passengers) or P&O Ferries (foot passengers and vehicles).

For drivers going between Dover and Benelux via France, you might want to read the driving page, and especially the section on road tolls and environmental zones.

Germany across the Ems-Dollard Estuary/Wadden Sea (from north to south):

  • Eemshaven – Borkum (Germany) with AG Ems. Foot passengers and vehicles. Timetable.
  • Delfzijl – Emden-Knock (Germany) – Borkum (Germany) with AG Ems. Foot passengers and bicycles. Operates in summer. Reservation compulsory.
  • Delfzijl – Emden (Germany) – Ditzum (Germany) with Internationale Dollard Route. Foot passengers and bicycles. Operates in summer.

Belgium across the Maas (from north to south):

  • Ohé en Laak – Ophoven (Flanders) with Rondje met het Pondje. Foot passengers and bicycles. Times. Operates in summer.
  • Grevenbicht – Rotem (Flanders) with Rondje met het Pondje. Foot passengers and bicycles. Times. Operates in summer.
  • Berg aan de Maas – Meeswijk (Flanders) with Ton Paulus Veerbedrijf. Mopeds, motorcycles, cars, trucks. Times.
  • Geulle – Uikhoven (Flanders) with Rondje met het Pondje. Foot passengers and bicycles. Times. Operates in summer.
  • Eijsden – Lanaye (Wallonia) with the Cramignon. Foot passengers and bicycles. Operates in summer.

You can see a list of (basically) all pedestrian/cyclist/motor-vehicle ferries in the Netherlands, Belgium, and Luxembourg at Vrienden van de Voetveren.

In Europe, there are numerous river cruises to/from Belgium and Germany, some even to/from France, Switzerland, and further (e.g. canal tours to/from the Danube). In the Caribbean, there are numerous cruise ships that visit Bonaire. See also details from the tourism websites of Sint Eustatius and Saba about getting there by boat.
