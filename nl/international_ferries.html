International ferries

Ferries to/from England:

  • IJmuiden (near Amsterdam) – Newcastle upon Tyne with DFDS Seaway. Foot passengers and vehicles.
  • Hoek van Holland (near Rotterdam) – Harwich with Stena Line. Foot passengers and vehicles. Also has a sail+rail deal with the Dutch railway network and/or the Greater Anglia network in England. Hoek van Holland terminal is served by the Hoek van Holland Haven train station, and Harwich terminal is served by the Harwich International train station.
  • Europoort Rotterdam – Hull with P&O Ferries. Foot passengers and vehicles.
  • Zeebrugge (Belgium) – Hull with P&O Ferries. Foot passengers and vehicles. (Walking from the ferry terminal to the two Belgian train stations in Zeebrugge not recommended; the port area is busy with heavy-duty trucks, and pedestrian facilities are not great.)
  • Dunkerque (Dunkirk; France) – Dover with DFDS Seaway. Vehicles (incl. bicycles) and its passengers.
  • Calais (France) – Dover with DFDS Seaway (Vehicles (incl. bicycles) and its passengers) or P&O Ferries (foot passengers and vehicles).

For drivers going between Dover and Benelux via France, you might want to read the driving page, and especially the section on road tolls and environmental zones.

Short distance ferries to/from Germany:

  • Eemshaven – Borkum (Germany) with AG Ems. Foot passengers and vehicles. Timetable.
  • Delfzijl – Emden (Germany) – Ditzum (Germany) with Internationale Dollard Route. Foot passengers and bicycles. Operates in summer.
  • Delfzijl – Emden-Knock (Germany) – Borkum (Germany) with AG Ems. Foot passengers and bicycles. Operates in summer. Reservation compulsory.

In Europe, there are numerous river cruises to/from Belgium and Germany, some even to/from France, Switzerland, or further (e.g. canal tours to/from the Danube). In the Caribbean, there are numerous cruise ships that visit Bonaire. See also details from the tourism websites of Sint Eustatius and Saba about getting there by boat.
