International ferries

North Sea

Ferries to England, across the North Sea/English Channel (from north to south):

  • IJmuiden (west of Amsterdam) – Newcastle upon Tyne (England) with DFDS Seaway. Foot passengers and vehicles. There is a coach/bus service between IJmuiden and Amsterdam, and between North Shields Port and Newcastle. In IJmuiden, there is also bus 82 connecting the ferry terminal to Amsterdam Sloterdijk (limited service; printed timetable; journey planner, input 'Sluisplein DFDS, IJmuiden' as the origin/destination).
  • Hoek van Holland (west of Rotterdam) – Harwich (England) with Stena Line. Foot passengers and vehicles. Hoek van Holland terminal is served by the Hoek van Holland Haven train station, and Harwich terminal is served by the Harwich International train station. There is also a Rail & Sail package (tickets from England, tickets from the Netherlands; note that the Netherlands webpage in English does not necessarily work properly). One option includes ferry and train travel in the destination country, and the other option includes ferry and train travel in both the origin and destination countries. (With the correct Rail & Sail ticket) you can travel between Hoek van Holland Haven and any station on Nederlandse Spoorwegen trains (not including Intercity direct and Thalys; see here) in the Netherlands, and/or between Harwich International and any station on Abellio Greater Anglia trains. In Hoek van Holland, another option is bus 35 to Den Haag Leyenburg, and then tram 4 or 6 or other buses to other destinations in Den Haag (The Hague) (journey planner).
  • Rotterdam Europoort – Hull (England) with P&O Ferries. Foot passengers and vehicles. There is a coach service between Rotterdam Europoort and Rotterdam, and between Hull Port and Hull. (There is no public transport anywhere near the Europoort P&O terminal.)
  • Zeebrugge (Belgium) – Hull (England) with P&O Ferries. Foot passengers and vehicles. There is a coach service between Zeebrugge terminal and Brugge, and between Hull Port and Hull. (Walking from the Zeebrugge ferry terminal to train/tram/bus stops in Zeebrugge not recommended; the port area is busy with trucks, and pedestrian facilities are not great.)
  • Dunkerque (Dunkirk; France) – Dover (England) with DFDS Seaway. Vehicles (incl. bicycles) and their passengers.
  • Calais (France) – Dover (England) with DFDS Seaway (Vehicles (incl. bicycles) and their passengers) or P&O Ferries (foot passengers and vehicles).

Other than the passenger services outlined above, there are also freight services for trucks and their drivers between Netherlands/Belgium and Britain/Scandinavia. Some openly advertise that they have limited space for passenger vehicles and their occupants. (For the others, I suppose you can ask them if you are very interested.)

  • DFDS:
    • Rotterdam Vlaardingen – Immingham (England)
    • Rotterdam Vlaardingen – Flexistowe (England)
    • Zeebrugge (Belgium) – Rosyth (Scotland)
    • Gent (Belgium) – Brevik (Norway)
    • Gent (Belgium) – Gothenburg (Sweden)
  • P&O:
    • Rotterdam Europoort – Teesport (England)
    • Zeebrugge (Belgium) – Teesport (England)
    • Zeebrugge (Belgium) – Tilbury (England)

For drivers and motorcyclists going between Calais/Dunkirk and Benelux, you might want to read the section on road tolls and environmental zones in the driving page for French road tolls.

Germany

Ferries to Germany across the Ems-Dollard Estuary/Wadden Sea (from north to south):

  • Eemshaven – Borkum (Germany) with AG Ems. Foot passengers and vehicles. Timetable. In summer, car traffic is restricted on Borkum. Ferry passengers get a free ride on the Borkumer Kleinbahn.
  • Delfzijl – Emden-Knock (Germany) – Borkum (Germany) with AG Ems. Foot passengers and bicycles. Operates in summer. Reservation compulsory.
  • Delfzijl – Emden (Germany) – Ditzum (Germany) with Dollard Route. Foot passengers and bicycles. Operates in summer.

Belgium

Ferries to Belgium across the Maas (from north to south):

  • Ohé en Laak – Ophoven (Flanders) with Rondje met het Pondje. Foot passengers and bicycles. Timetable. Operates in summer.
  • Grevenbicht – Rotem (Flanders) with Rondje met het Pondje. Foot passengers and bicycles. Timetable. Operates in summer.
  • Berg aan de Maas – Meeswijk (Flanders) with Ton Paulus Veerbedrijf. Mopeds, motorcycles, cars, trucks. Timetable.
  • Geulle – Uikhoven (Flanders) with Rondje met het Pondje. Foot passengers and bicycles. Timetable. Operates in summer.
  • Eijsden – Lanaye (Wallonia) with the Cramignon. Foot passengers and bicycles. Operates in summer.

You can see a list of all public foot passenger/bicycle/motor-vehicle ferries in the Netherlands, Belgium, and Luxembourg at Vrienden van de Voetveren.

In Europe, there are numerous river cruises to/from Belgium and Germany, some even to/from France, Switzerland, and further (e.g. canal tours to/from the Danube). In the Caribbean, there are numerous cruise ships that visit Bonaire. See also details from the tourism websites of Sint Eustatius and Saba about getting there by boat.
