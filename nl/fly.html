Passenger flights

The primary gateway of the Netherlands is Amsterdam Schiphol Airport AMS ( English | Dutch | German | French | Swedish | Russian | Spanish | Italian | Simplified Chinese ). In the last few years, it has been the fifth or sixth busiest airport in the world in terms of international passenger numbers. The passenger numbers through Schiphol dwarfs all the other airports in the Netherlands. It has its own underground train station (Schiphol Airport), with direct international trains to Belgium and France, and to Germany via easy connections elsewhere on the Dutch railway network.

The second busiest airport is Eindhoven Airport EIN ( English | Dutch ). It is a large base for low-cost and leisure carriers, offering many short haul flights to Europe, Anatolia, and Maghreb.

The third busiest airport is Rotterdam The Hauge Airport RTM ( English | Dutch ). Other than budget and leisure carriers, the airport also attracts some traditional carriers, with some of the routes buisness-oriented.

Two smaller airports with regular passenger flights in European Netherlands are Groningen Airport GRQ ( English | Dutch | German | Polish ) in the northeast, and Maastricht Aachen Airport MST ( English | Dutch | German | French ) in the southeast.

Other options include the airports in nearby countries. Many of them are a quick drive away from the Dutch border, and some have reasonable public transport links to the Netherlands. The following airports in Germany and Belgium have webpages in Dutch. For the German airports, it shows that they get a significant number of customers from the Netherlands; for the Belgian airports, because Belgium).

Germany:

  • Weeze Airport NRN ( English | Dutch | German ). On the Dutch border, approximately equidistant from Nijmegen and Venlo. Often marketed as Düsseldorf Weeze Airport. Low-cost and leisure carriers.
  • Düsseldorf Airport DUS ( English | Dutch | German ). West of Venlo and Roermond. Third busiest airport in Germany in terms of passenger numbers. Be very careful that there are two different train stations for this airport: Düsseldorf Flughafen Terminal, and Düsseldorf Flughafen. Check very carefully which one you are meant to board your intended train at.
  • Cologne Bonn Airport CGN ( English | Dutch | German ). West of Maastricht/Heerlen. Another big airport, with its own train station (Köln/Bonn Flughafen).
  • Dortmund Airport DTM ( English | Dutch | German | Polish ). Further west than Düsseldorf. Low-cost and leisure carriers.
  • Münster Osnabrück Airport FMO ( English | Dutch | German ). West of Enschede. Low-cost and leisure carriers.

Belgium:

  • Antwerp Airport ANR ( English | Dutch ). South of Roosendaal. A mix of leisure flights, buisness-oriented flights, and other regional flights.
  • Ostend–Bruges Airport OST ( English | Dutch | French ). Soutwest of the Southwestern coast of the Netherlands. Mostly leisure flights.
  • Brussels Airport BRU ( English | Dutch | French ). South of Antwerp. Major international airport, similar in passenger numbers as Düsseldorf Airport. It has its own underground railway station (Brussels Airport-Zaventem).
  • Charleroi Airport CRL ( English | Dutch | French ). South of Brussels, often marketed as Brussels South Airport. Major hub for low-cost and leisure carriers.
  • Liège Airport LGG ( English | Dutch | French | German ). South of Maastricht. Leisure carriers. (Liège Airport LGG is much more important as a cargo airport than a passenger airport.)

The competition for Dutch customers from the Rhine-Rhur area airports is strong enough that KLM provides two free buses pre day to/from Schiphol for customers in Arnhem/Nijmegen and Eindhoven/Maastricht.

There are no domestic flights in the Netherlands. The shortest flights from Amsterdam AMS are to Brussels BRU and Düsseldorf DUS.

In Caribbean Netherlands, each of the three islands has its own airport:

  • Bonaire Airport BON. Direct flights from Amsterdam and some cities in USA. Otherwise flights are mostly to neighbouring Curaçao CUR, and sometimes Aruba AUA, which are much larger airports. How to get there, from the Bonaire tourism website.
  • Sint Eustatius Airport EUX is small. You can get there from Sint Maarten SXM. How to get there, from the Sint Eustatius tourism website.
  • Saba Airport Airport SAB is small. The runway is frighteningly/excitingly very small. You can get there from Sint Maarten SXM. How to get there, from the Saba tourism website.

Private jets, charter flights, DIY

There is an abundance of Dutch and foreign private jet and charter companies. Amsterdam Schiphol and Rotterdam The Hague Airports have dedicated private jet terminals. You might also want to do you own flying. Other than the airports outlined above, the following are some smaller airports in the Netherlands, of various sizes:

  • Fryslân: Ameland Ballum EHAL
  • Fryslân: Drachten EHDR
  • Groningen: Oostwold EHOW
  • Groningen: Stadskanaal EHST
  • Drenthe: Hoogeveen EHHO
  • Overijssel: Enschede Twente EHTW
  • Gelderland: Teuge EHTE
  • Flevoland: Lelystad EHLE
  • Noord-Holland: Texel EHTE
  • Noord-Holland: Den Helder de Kooy EHKD
  • Noord-Holland: Middenmeer
  • Noord-Holland: Hilversum EHHV
  • Zeeland: Middelburg Midden Zeeland EHMZ
  • Noord-Brabant: Breda Seppe EHSE
  • Noord-Brabant: Kempen EHBD
  • Limburg: Venlo EHVE

As for heliport, there is at least the Amsterdam Heliport that is not military or private-private. As far as I know, there are no fixed-route commuter helicopter services in the Netherlands. (The Netherlands is not the Pearl River Delta or the Gulf of Finland.) There are many helicopter companies where you can charter a helicopter to land, e.g., on an oil rig, in the middle of the opening of your own concert at an open-air stadium.

Zweefvliegen 'gliding' is also popular in the Netherlands. There are many zweefvliegvelden 'glider airfields' around the country.
